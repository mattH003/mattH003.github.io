Hello and Welcome to my Website!

My name is Matthieu Hazard, I am a French-American Mechanical engineering student at Northeastern University. My passions lie in forging groundbreaking technologies, with an interest in the realms of rocketry, energy, aviation and robotics.

I am particularly interested in propulsion engineering and fluids and I currently lead the Liquid Rocket Engine (LRE) Team here at Northeastern. Working with this 20-30 person team has been a wild ride as we developed, manufactured and tested a bi-propellant liquid rocket engine in less than 6 months! We’re all currently working on finalizing the engine and building a flight vehicle with a prospective launch date of Spring 2027.

Apart from the LRE team I have been involved in propulsion through Parker Aerospace where I worked as an R&D mechanical engineering co-op developing and testing vane and gerotor lube/scavenge pumps for jet engines built by GE aerospace, Rolls Royce and Pratt and Whitney.

Outside of propulsion, I’ve completed two additional co-ops at C&K Components and Thermacell, where I gained hands-on experience designing and testing mechanical systems, switches, and custom test equipment.

Upon completing my undergraduate degree I plan on pursuing a masters in aerospace engineering with a focus in propulsion systems. I’m also actively seeking a propulsion engineering internship for Summer 2026 following my graduation in April.
