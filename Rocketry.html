Seraphim engine Ablative Version

Working on the design and analysis of the ablative version of the Seraphim engine. Utilizing CFD to fully characterize nozzle outflow and developping an ablation model using the combustion species to determine the total recession rate of the graphite nozzle throat to characterize the chages in A* throughtout the engine burn time. The main purpose of this project is to finalize the engine design that will be manufactured and tested in the Spring 2026 semester.

Horizontal Stand Engine Mounting

Did all the structural calculations for the new mounting plate of the horizontal stand's load cell carriage, analyzing the factor of safety against buckling and yielding using FEA simulations and hand caculations. Designed and implemented the new plate with a secondary support structure to minize the moment on the standoff's holding the engine.

Engine Heat Transfer Python Code

Updated a legacy Python script developed by a former team member to enable plotting of additional nodes along the engine profile and incorporated new equations to fully parameterize the geometry. This allowed iterative optimization of the converging section for improved heat transfer and reduced engine weight while maintaining thermal integrity.
